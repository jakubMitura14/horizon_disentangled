The Multidisciplinary Workflow of Prostate Cancer Diagnosis

A complex, iterative process requiring multimodal data fusion and expert collaboration.

1. Patient Presentation & Initial Data

The process begins with collecting foundational patient information, which forms the basis for all subsequent investigations.

Risk Factors (Age, Genetics) Clinical & Patient History Lab Results (e.g., PSA)

2. Multimodal Investigation Loop

Specialists perform various investigations. The order is not fixed and often depends on the initial findings, creating an iterative diagnostic loop.

Data Fusion Point

All findings converge for synthesis.

MRI

Standard for local staging, detecting tumor extent, and capsular/seminal vesicle invasion.

PSMA PET/CT

Gold standard for detecting nodal and distant metastases with high sensitivity.

Histopathology

Definitive diagnosis via biopsy, providing Gleason Score and confirming aggressive features.

Contrast CT & Other

Visualizes bone structure, assesses systemic risks (body composition) & post-therapy response (SPECT/CT).

3. Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Review

A "Tumor Board" of specialists convenes to synthesize all fused data, discuss findings, and formulate a consensus plan.

Radiologist Urologist Oncologist Pathologist

4. Comprehensive Staging & Report

The final output is a detailed report providing a definitive diagnosis, precise cancer staging (TNM), risk stratification, and a recommended treatment plan.

A Significant Clinical Challenge

This entire workflow is intricate and labor-intensive. The need to integrate multiple data types across different specialties at various time points presents a major bottleneck, highlighting the demand for streamlined, intelligent solutions.
