Risk Factors & Premalignant

Cellular changes driven by risk

Asymptomatic. Risk factors (age, family history). Baseline PSA.
No tumor visible. Can quantify systemic risks (fat, sarcopenia).
Not used for risk factors. May show benign changes like BPH.
Not indicated. No PSMA expression.
Biopsy may show precursors (HGPIN, ASAP).

Organ-Confined Disease

Cancer (T1/T2) in prostate

Asymptomatic, but elevated PSA. Post-RP, PSA should be zero.
Cannot visualize organ-confined tumors. No role in local staging.
Standard for detection: low T2 signal, low ADC, early enhancement.
Visualizes primary tumor. High SUVmax/PSMA-TV suggests aggressiveness.
Definitive diagnosis via biopsy (adenocarcinoma, Gleason Score).

Aggressive Histology

Transition to high-grade

No unique symptoms. High PSA, rapid kinetics (short PSADT).
Not visible. CT Perfusion may show high Blood Flow (angiogenesis).
Lower ADC values. Radiomics can quantify heterogeneity (cribriform).
High PSMA-TV on staging predicts cribriform/LVI.
Gold Standard: visualization of cribriform/IDC-P (Gleason 4).

Local Invasion

Extracapsular Extension (T3/T4)

Local symptoms (urinary/erectile). Rising PSA, short PSADT.
Low sensitivity for micro ECE. Can show gross extension (T3/T4).
Crucial for staging: shows capsular disruption, SVI.
Detects PSMA-avid foci beyond capsule, improving ECE/SVI staging.
Definitive via RP: confirms ECE or SVI microscopically.

Regional Spread

Lymph Node Metastasis (N1)

Asymptomatic. Main sign is BCR (rising PSA post-therapy).
Low sensitivity N-staging; relies on size (>1cm), misses micro-mets.
Better than CT; uses size, shape, low ADC. Still limited.
New Gold Standard: high accuracy for small nodes via molecular signature.
Definitive via histology: confirms positive nodes and ENE.

Distant Dissemination

Bone Metastasis (M1b)

Symptomatic (bone pain). Elevated AP, anemia, detectable CTCs.
Excellent for visualizing osteoblastic lesions and fracture risk.
Highly sensitive for early detection (marrow replacement).
More sensitive than bone scan; quantifies total tumor burden.
Bone biopsy confirms mets, but not always needed if imaging is clear.
