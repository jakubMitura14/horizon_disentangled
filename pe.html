Risk Factors & Premalignant State

Cellular changes driven by risk factors

Asymptomatic. Family history, age, ethnicity. Baseline PSA, risk calculators.
Cannot visualize tumor. Can quantify systemic risks: visceral fat, sarcopenia.
Not typically used for risk factors alone. May show benign changes (BPH).
Not indicated. No PSMA expression in precursor lesions.
Biopsy may show precursor lesions like High-Grade PIN or ASAP.

Organ-Confined Disease

Early cancer (T1/T2) within the prostate capsule

Asymptomatic. Elevated PSA is key. Post-RP, PSA should be undetectable.
Cannot visualize organ-confined tumors. No role in local staging.
Standard for detection. Shows tumor as low T2 signal, low ADC, and early enhancement.
Visualizes primary tumor. High SUVmax & PSMA-TV correlate with aggressiveness.
Definitive diagnosis. Biopsy confirms adenocarcinoma & Gleason Score.

Aggressive Histological Features

Transition to high-grade disease

No unique symptoms. Often higher PSA, rapid PSA kinetics (short PSADT).
Not directly visible. CT Perfusion may show increased angiogenesis (high Blood Flow).
Progressively lower ADC values. Radiomics can quantify heterogeneity linked to cribriform pattern.
High PSMA-TV on staging scans is predictive of cribriform pattern and LVI.
Gold Standard. Direct visualization of cribriform architecture or IDC-P (Gleason Pattern 4).

Local Invasion

Extracapsular Extension (T3/T4)

Onset of local urinary/erectile symptoms. Rising PSA, short PSADT.
Low sensitivity for microscopic ECE. Can depict gross extension (T3/T4) into fat/bladder.
Crucial for staging. Shows capsular disruption, SVI. Length of capsular contact assesses risk.
Can detect PSMA-avid tumor foci extending beyond the capsule, improving staging of ECE/SVI.
Definitive diagnosis on RP. Microscopic confirmation of tumor beyond capsule (ECE) or in seminal vesicles (SVI).

Regional Spread

Lymph Node Metastasis (N1)

Usually asymptomatic. Primary sign is Biochemical Recurrence (BCR) - rising PSA post-therapy.
Standard for N-staging but low sensitivity. Relies on node size (>1cm), missing micrometastases.
Better than CT. Detects suspicious nodes by size, shape, low ADC. Still limited for small nodes.
New Gold Standard. High accuracy for detecting small, metastatic nodes based on molecular signature, not size.
Definitive confirmation. Histology of dissected nodes confirms number of positive nodes & extranodal extension (ENE).

Distant Dissemination

Bone Metastasis (M1b)

Symptomatic. Bone pain is most common. Elevated AP, anemia. Detectable CTCs.
Excellent for visualization. Clearly shows high-density osteoblastic lesions and assesses fracture risk.
Highly sensitive for early detection. Shows replacement of bright fatty marrow with dark tumor signal.
Higher sensitivity than bone scan. Visualizes viable tumor cells and quantifies total metastatic burden.
Bone biopsy confirms metastatic adenocarcinoma, but not always required if imaging is clear.

Therapeutic Interventions Altering Disease Evolution

Radical Prostatectomy

Removes the primary tumor in localized disease to prevent spread and provide definitive staging.

Clinical & Lab

Post-op PSA should become undetectable. A rising PSA (≥0.2 ng/ml) defines Biochemical Recurrence (BCR), the first sign of treatment failure.

Histopathology

Gold standard for prognosis. Provides definitive pathological stage (pT), Gleason Score, and confirms adverse features (ECE, SVI), margin status (PSM), and nodal status (pN).

Functional Outcomes

Prognosis also defined by Quality of Life. High rates of erectile dysfunction and urinary incontinence can occur, which must be balanced against oncological benefits.

Hormonal Therapy (ADT)

A systemic therapy for advanced disease that targets the androgen receptor (AR) signaling axis.

Clinical & Lab

Leads to initial sharp decline in PSA. Long-term prognosis is linked to PSA nadir (lowest level) and time-to-nadir; a high nadir or short time predicts earlier resistance.

Imaging (CT/PET/MRI)

Causes body composition changes on CT (muscle loss, fat gain). PSMA-PET uptake decreases (but can flare initially). MRI can show reduced citrate, reflecting cell atrophy.

Induced Evolution

Inevitably selects for resistance, leading to lethal Castration-Resistant PCa (CRPC) through AR-dependent or AR-independent (e.g., Neuroendocrine) pathways.

Radioligand Therapy (RLT)

A targeted approach for advanced mCRPC, delivering radiation directly to PSMA-positive cancer cells.

Mechanism of Action

Uses ligands (e.g., ¹⁷⁷Lu-PSMA-617) to deliver a radioactive isotope directly to tumor cells, causing targeted cell death with minimal collateral damage.

Imaging & Selection

PSMA-PET is essential to confirm high PSMA expression on tumors, identifying patients who are likely to respond to the therapy (a "theranostic" approach).

Prognostic Impact

Significantly prolongs survival and delays progression in heavily pre-treated mCRPC patients, altering the terminal course of the disease.
